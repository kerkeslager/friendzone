Principles

FriendZone is built on the following principles:

  • FriendZone is a tool for people who already know each other. FriendZone is not for finding friends. FriendZone is not for businesses or organizations. FriendZone is not for accumulating vast numbers of "friends".
  • The primary scope of interaction is one-on-one. FriendZone is not for large groups. The top priority of FriendZone is facilitating one-on-one interactions.
  • FriendZone should not concern itself with popularity or engagement. FriendZone will never support "likes", and does not have the goal of getting more users, or getting existing users to use the site more. Sometimes users are best served by logging off.
  • Users control who has access to their data. When users submit data to our site, it should be clear who will have access to that data, and we should never share data with anyone else (even with permission; for this reason we should never ask users for permission to use their data). The one exception is that we will follow applicable laws; however, we will make an effort to store user data in such a way that even we do not have access to it (so that even the law cannot obligate us to share it).
  • Users can delete their data. Users should always be able to delete their data from our servers. When a user deletes their data, it should be actually deleted, so that it is not recoverable by anyone, including us.
  • Money comes from users only. To make sure that our incentives are always aligned with serving users, we will always be supported by user donations. We will never commit to costs beyond what our users have committed to donate, and we will limit commitments we accept to avoid becoming dependent on just a few users.
  • Donations should never be required to use features of FriendZone. Donations are always voluntary.
  • Where possible, we don't require users to trust us. Our code will always be free (as in freedom). We should not require JavaScript to user our site. As we can, we'll implement features that allow users to encrypt their data with their own encryption keys so that even we can't access it.
  • FriendZone will shut down rather than compromise these values. Many organizations which are founded with good intentions drift away from their initial values through a series of small compromises which are each intended to keep the organization alive. We strive to keep vigilant of value drift and never compromise our values, even if that means shutting down FriendZone.
