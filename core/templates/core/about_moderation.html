Moderation

Our moderation policy is simple: we don't moderate, users do.

There are a number of reasons for this policy:

  • We value our mental health. There are many cases on other sites where moderators are traumatized by the horrific content people post online. We don't want to see that stuff, and we don't think it would be ethical to ask anyone else to look at that stuff.
  • FriendZone is for people who already know each other. If you don't like the content someone is sending you, you are encouraged to set boundaries with that person about what kind of content they can send you, and disconnect from them if they don't respect your boundaries. FriendZone is not in the business of setting or enforcing our users' boundaries.
  • Moderation inherently involves violating the privacy of our users. The general model of moderation is that a user uploads some data, which may have the expectation of privacy, and another user flags that data for moderation. This is one person uploading data with the expectation of privacy, and another person cannot consent to the first person's giving up the privacy of that data.
  • Leaving moderation up to users allows us to keep our team small. Users who donate to FriendZone should be able to expect that their donations will be used efficiently.

Some content is illegal, or evidence of a crime. If you receive such content, you are encouraged to report it to the relevant authorities. We will comply with laws with regard to illegal content.
